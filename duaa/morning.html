Duaa 1

Entering the Morning

Acknowledging that life, death, and our return are all by Allah.

Recite once

Arabic

اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا، وَبِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا، وَبِكَ نَحْيَا، وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ، وَإِلَيْكَ النُّشُورُ

Transliteration

Allāhumma bika aṣbaḥnā, wa bika amsaynā, wa bika naḥyā, wa bika namūtu, wa ilaykan-nushūr.

English

O Allah, by You we enter the morning and by You we enter the evening; by You we live and by You we die, and to You is the resurrection.

Source: Jāmiʿ at-Tirmidhī 3391; graded ḥasan.

Duaa 2

Sayyid al-Istighfār

The most excellent supplication for seeking Allah’s forgiveness.

Recite once

Arabic

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ، وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ

Transliteration

Allāhumma anta Rabbī, lā ilāha illā anta. Khalaqtanī wa ana ʿabduka, wa ana ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mā istaṭaʿtu. Aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿtu. Abūʾu laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya, wa abūʾu laka bidhanbī, faghfir lī, fa-innahū lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā anta.

English

O Allah, You are my Lord. None has the right to be worshipped except You. You created me and I am Your servant, and I remain faithful to Your covenant and promise as much as I am able. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favor upon me, and I acknowledge my sin, so forgive me, for none forgives sins except You.

Source: Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 6306.

Duaa 3

Contentment with Allah, Islam, and the Prophet ﷺ

Affirming contentment with Allah as Lord, Islam as religion, and Muhammad ﷺ as Prophet.

Recite three times

Arabic

رَضِيتُ بِاللَّهِ رَبًّا، وَبِالْإِسْلَامِ دِينًا، وَبِمُحَمَّدٍ ﷺ نَبِيًّا

Transliteration

Raḍītu billāhi Rabban, wa bil-Islāmi dīnan, wa bi-Muḥammadin ﷺ Nabiyyan.

English

I am pleased with Allah as my Lord, with Islam as my religion, and with Muhammad ﷺ as my Prophet.

Source: Hisn al-Muslim 87; reported by Aḥmad, an-Nasāʾī, and at-Tirmidhī.

Duaa 4

Protection from Harm

Seeking protection in Allah’s Name from harm in the earth and the heavens.

Recite three times

Arabic

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ، وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ

Transliteration

Bismillāhilladhī lā yaḍurru maʿa ismihi shayʾun fil-arḍi wa lā fis-samāʾi, wa Huwas-Samīʿul-ʿAlīm.

English

In the Name of Allah, with Whose Name nothing on earth or in heaven can cause harm, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.

Source: Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5088 and Jāmiʿ at-Tirmidhī 3388.
